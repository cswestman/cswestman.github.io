Experience

  • Background

    Born and raised in Southern Florida, I eventually moved to North Georgia, graduating high school at Ringgold High, just South of Chattanooga, Tennessee. Upon graduation, I joined the Army. I served for eight years, ended my term, then served as an Operations Manager at an electrical supply warehouse. During this last year, I have spent most of my down time reading books, learning coding languages, and building interesting technologies. This entire journey, rather brief, has led me to gain a lot of useful and exciting skills.

  • Web Design and Development Skills

    Let me get right to the point! Throughout this learning and development process, I must say, I’ve come to the realization that I absolutely love this technology and the power behind it. Listed below, are the skills I’ve attained over the last couple of years.

    Development using coding languages such as CSS3, HTML5, JavaScript, and even a hint of PHP, C#, and Java

    Using the jQuery library to help streamline development workflows by simplifying code

    Building responsive web content built on UI and UX principles, reacting and changing based on screen size and orientation

    Using mobile first approaches when creating content primarily being viewed on mobile devices

    Designing content based on graphic design principles and theories, and using Adobe InDesign and Photoshop to help generate design concepts

    Creating/editing images and icons using Adobe Creative programs, such as Photoshop and Illustrator

  • Project and Operational Management Skills

    Throughout my time in the Army, and while also serving as an Operations Manager in my civilian life, I have gained many great skills that allow me to bring teams together, streamline operational needs, and complete projects on time. I did this all using the following:

    Managing logistical supply needs of warehouse and offices

    Coordinating incoming and outgoing freight

    Managing sales and marketing strategies

    Refining operational processes through careful process analysis and feedback loops

    Managing projects using SCRUM principles

    Maintaining team morale through event planning and weekly counseling and feedback

    Cross training team members

  • Training and Instructional Skills

    A large portion of my Army service was spent training Soldiers. I did this through small unit training while serving as a Squad Leader and Platoon Sergeant at various units. Additionally, I did this on an official level, as a certified Army instructor. When serving at Fort Benning, I was a full-time instructor teaching and certifying Soldiers. I taught Pathfinder, Air Assault, and Rappel Master courses. To accomplish everything talked about, I used the following skills:

    Adaptive teaching techniques to cater towards multiple learning styles

    Coordinate and request all instruction supply needs

    Plan and update course scope and execution methods based on updated course standards and regulations

    Train subordinate leadership on the fundamentals of effective teaching

    Use course critique feedback loops to improve and refine course material and execution

    Prepare and rehearse instruction

    Confident public speaking
